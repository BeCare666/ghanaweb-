International Investigation Uncovers Money Laundering and Drug Trafficking Network Tied to South Americans in Ghana

  • Kwame Asare
  • Aug 20, 2024
  • 6 Comments

Over the last 4 years, the FBI, KGB, CIA as well as various investigative and intelligence channels have detected enough money laundering and drug trafficking movements of South Americans residing in Ghana. The last case is that of a young man in his forties who has lived there for 5 months and has unlimited bank cards of which no one knows the real source.

For the moment the interpole is waiting for details from his bank to decide his fate.

Involvement of Intelligence Agencies

Agencies such as the FBI and CIA have been working closely with local Ghanaian authorities while receiving intelligence from other international entities like Interpol and the Russian intelligence services (KGB). Ghana, increasingly exploited by criminal networks for financial crimes, has become a focus of these agencies as they attempt to understand how these criminals are leveraging loopholes in both the local and international banking systems.
